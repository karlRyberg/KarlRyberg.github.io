Political Situation

Answer

We see CNG as a transitional fuel, meaning that it in the long-term will be replaced with even more environmentally friendly fuels such as electricity. Even so, it is the most mature solution currently available. This is also reflected in the current political climate regarding CNG!

For instance, it is clear that there is support for developing the CNG infrastructure, both from the market and politically. In Europe, a directive was decided on in 2014 requiring member states to improve their alternative fuel infrastructure including CNG. The requirement states that there should be a sufficient number of refuelling stations, ideally every 150 km, to enable operation of CNG vehicles in urban and suburban areas.

Political Situation

Answer (cont.)

In addition to this, politicians look at other ways to enable the transition towards more environmentally friendly fuels. The CO2 regulation in the EU is one way of doing this, highlighting which trucks that are most favourable in terms of tailpipe emissions. Several countries are taking legislative action to ease the energy transition. For instance, public purchasers are in some countries required to consider environmental impact when purchasing vehicles. There are also countries which require that vehicles with low environmental impact are selected in tender processes. All in all, this favours CNG solutions!
