RENAULT TRUCK'S STRATEGY

Reducing CO2 emissions and environmental impact is a high priority for us. Today, there is no perfect alternative to substitute diesel in all applications and all markets in an efficient manner. Therefore, we believe in a long-term transition from fossil fuels to renewable fuels where we utilize a mix of energy sources to reduce CO2 and pollutant emissions.

Our strategy differs between urban transport and regional and long haul transport. Click on the operation icons to read more about what we strive towards in terms of alternative fuels within the different segments.

LONG HAUL AND REGIONAL TRANSPORT

For regional and long distance transport, we believe that the best solution to reduce the environmental footprint is to remain focused on reducing fuel consumption. Due to the demands on energy efficiency, profitability and operational flexibility, we see that diesel will remain the dominant energy source in the foreseeable future.

In addition to fuel consumption reduction, we see great potential in using synthetic fuels like HVO to reduce CO2 emissions from a well-to-wheel perspective. That is why all our HD and MD engines are now 100 % compatible with these fuels.

Furthermore, we will progressively electrify our trucks through specific functions and auxiliaries.

URBAN TRANSPORT

For shorter distances, or closer to urbanized areas, we see CNG as a replacement for diesel. With political pressure to find alternatives to diesel for cities, CNG solutions are increasingly considered. We have sold CNG trucks since 2004, and in 2017 more than 700 of our CNG trucks were on the roads.

Despite this, we see CNG as a transitional fuel. It still emits pollutants and greenhouse gases, and it requires extensive infrastructure deployment.
